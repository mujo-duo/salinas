Helping the World One Crop at a Time

This project was done as part of the NASA SpaceApps challenge. We used satellite data to monitor affected crops in Salinas due to COVID-19.

Find Out More

Problem Statement

One of the significant consequences of the sudden shutdown is the millions of pounds of unharvested food rotting on farms while demand for emergency food assistance increased by over 73%. The USDA announced the coronavirus food assistance program one month after the shelter in place to aid farmers, ranchers, and consumers, a program that farmers and other critics say was too late. The waste, food insecurity, and delay in providing assistance revealed the fragility and lack of flexibility in an extraordinarily complex food system. There is a need to create a more resilient food system that can better address the sudden and dramatic shifts caused by the pandemic.

Approach

We utilized earth observation data (from Landsat-8) and remote sensing techniques to identify vegetation areas of u­­nharvested food on farms. We then combined the derived map with crop data to determine yearly harvest patterns of major crops cultivated in Salinas. The wide availability of real-time satellite imagery with high spatial and temporal resolution means more accurate and up-to-date measurements of farm food loss to inform agricultural policy. We developed the entire data processing pipeline using Google Earth Engine.
